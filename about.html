About G.V. Kumar

Meteorologist • Researcher • Writer

Background

Having graduated from Madras Christian College, Chennai in 1972 in Science, G.V. Kumar joined India Meteorological Department (IMD), Regional Meteorological Centre (RMC), Chennai on 31 December 1974.

He served the department for nearly 37 years, dedicating his career to advancing meteorological science in India. He retired from service on 31 January 2012 as Meteorologist B from RMC Chennai on superannuation.

Professional Expertise

G.V. Kumar has rich experience in:

  • Inspection, installation and calibration of meteorological instruments
  • Establishment of meteorological observatories
  • Weather observation and data analysis
  • Climate science research and documentation

Published Research

He contributed a research article to Mausam (formerly known as Journal of Meteorology and Atmospheric Sciences):

“Global Solar Radiation Derived from Sunshine Data”
Mausam, 1988, Vol. 39, No. 1, pp. 87–90

Current Work

In retirement, G.V. Kumar continues to apply his deep understanding of atmospheric science to contemporary environmental issues. His recent article on Delhi's winter smog crisis brings together decades of meteorological expertise to explain the complex chemistry, health impacts, and potential solutions for one of India's most pressing environmental challenges.
